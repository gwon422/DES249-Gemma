
## Experiment I Found Most Interesting
The experiment I found most interesting was the Week 2 Interactive Data Portrait. I found this experiment engaging because it encouraged me to think about how I could collect and represent my own data in a way that other people could interact with. Rather than working with anonymous or large-scale datasets, the focus on personal data made the task feel more meaningful and reflective. Designing an interactive portrait also helped me understand how interaction can change the way data is read and experienced, turning static information into something participatory.

## Theme or Idea I Keep Coming Back To
A theme I keep returning to in my work is exploring different types of data and how they can be visually displayed in interactive ways. I am particularly interested in experimenting with non-traditional data sources, including personal, behavioural, or everyday data, and finding creative visual forms to represent them. Interactivity plays an important role in this theme, as I am drawn to projects where the audience can directly influence or explore the data rather than passively viewing it.

## Something I’m Curious About but Haven’t Explored Yet
One area I am curious about but have not yet had the chance to explore further is how interactive data visualisations can respond more dynamically to user input or change over time. I would like to experiment with adding animation or real-time updates to make data portraits feel more alive and responsive. Exploring these ideas would allow me to build on the foundations of the Interactive Data Portrait and further investigate how interaction can shape the way data is understood
